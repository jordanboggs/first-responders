ICAT: Integrating Communications, Assessment, and Tactics- Training Guide for Defusing Critical Situations

This ICAT Training Guide is focused on protecting officers in non-firearms incidents in terms of physical safety as well as wellness. This is accomplished by equipping officers with the tools and techniques needed to slow down some situations, and provide additional specific options for safely resolving them. The goal is to help officers avoid reaching the point where their lives or the lives of others become endangered and the officers have no choice but to use lethal force

The Police Executive Research Forum is in the process of finalizing the content for these six modules, based on feedback received during pilot testing of the Training Guide. In the near future, specific lesson plans, PowerPoint presentations, videos, scenario-based training scripts and other resources will be posted on this website: http://www.policeforum.org/icat-training-guide-materials

NAMI (National Alliance on Mental Illness) in Colorado

In Our Own Voices is an in-person public education program in which two trained presenters share compelling personal stories about living with mental illness and achieving recovery. Seeks to change the attitudes, preconceived notions and stereotypes of who and what persons living with mental illness look and act like.

Mental Health First Aid Colorado: Law Enforcement, Corrections, & Public Safety

Eight-hour in- person course specially designed for police officers, first responders, corrections officers and other public safety professionals, helping them better understand mental illnesses and addictions and providing them with effective response options to de-escalate incidents without compromising safety. The course itself is free; some agencies sponsoring the training charge for books and/or lunch on a per person basis.

Contact Information: ℅ Colorado Behavioral Healthcare Council, 1580 Logan Street Suite 400, Denver, CO 80203, 303-832-7594. Upcoming classes listed on website.

CITAC: Crisis Intervention Teams Association of Colorado

CITAC is a non-profit professional membership association representing Crisis Intervention Teams (CIT) officers and others involved in the criminal justice / mental health interface throughout Colorado. Our mission is to encourage networking and resource sharing among CIT officers and others with a professional interest in CIT statewide; to develop and broaden community partnerships and collaboration among first responders; and to support the ongoing professional education of CIT officers. CITAC is also charged with assisting agencies new to CIT with implementation of programs that conform with state standards.
